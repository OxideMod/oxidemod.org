    Click to expand...
    Older cupboards override newer cupboards, this has always been the way - in your scenario, if the second cupboard is the one they authed on, they would have no build privilege in the area of the first, older cupboard. If they authed on your first cupboard, they would have building privs in its entire coverage (and still denied in the coverage of the second which does not overlap the first).

    This is how Rust is designed and has been since cupboards started (AFAIK) - not a mod bug. Of course being able to modify this behaviour may be a useful mod in itself

    Well, I've seen the opposite happen, where someone's got building block everywhere... It's happened to me before too. The two main alternatives in code to defeat this bug, would be to either not allow teleports to execute when the player is within *any* cupboard's radius (pretty restrictive when you think about it), or to put in some kind of check/routine which 'resets' your privilege following a teleport (or a wake-up). The latter may still be exploitable, but it seems a lot less likely for exploits to be successful or useful.

    Edit: Oh, and I've seen the 'block stuck on' bug in Rust without mods, or at least without teleport mods, so it's likely that the underlying issue is with Rust's vanilla code not always properly updating priv status; in my case I believe it was simply that I lagged while leaving a building block area. The mods in this instance just make it easier to recreate the bug, sometimes with an advantage to the player (as in the example of the clan you mentioned). Should be something we can fix with mod code though!
