    For one thing do serverstatus command in console. Look at cycle rate and frame rate. You preferably want cycle rate to be over 1.00 and frame rate to be averaging 60 to 70. The "guaranteed cycle rate" can be ignored completely. It doesn't involve us

    To attempt to resolve low cycle rates (below 1.00) you use "loadbalancerframebudget value" in autoexec. Default is 100 so without having this in autoexec 100 is what it is at by default. What you want to do is increase in increments of 50. I did it every restart for about 10 days until I found a value that worked for me. This is a balancing act as if you put loadbalancerframebudget too high it will create low fps on your server. So each time you raise the number you want to wait until a good amount of people are logged in then use "serverstatus" command to make sure you didn't hurt FPS. You should also notice your cycle rates go up as you increase loadbalancerframebudget. Not all servers are capable of raising this much without dramatic effects on fps, however assuming hardware is good.... you should be able to use this to get the cycle rate over 1.00 at all times.... even with server full. Once this is achieved, many lag issues tend to disappear and lease stream pool errors are reduced - however these will never go away completely, nor are they intended on going away completely.

    Also note that this is one of the solutions which has worked for me. Your results may vary.
