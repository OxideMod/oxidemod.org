  5. Heatst0rm

    The thing is you cannot prevent all hacks at once some will work for lets say 3-5 days and then the user will be banned. (best case even before with the requirement that the hack is already detected)
    What you could do is get Minimum Playtime Checker for Rust | Oxide to prevent users who haven't played rust for atleast 2 hours to join.
    Most cheaters use the refund policy from steam to get rust cheat for 1 hr and 50 min and then refund it back so even if they get banned it doesn't matter.
    With that plugin you could get rid of those players at least.
    For the moment you would need custom hooks to check specific stuff. ESP you cannot prevent through a plugin that is the job of EAC and it works quite fine. There will always be a fight between hacks and the EAC. Some private hacks will work for a long time since there are some private hacks which are only used for a very small group making the detection even harder and even more those hacks aren't really for sale so devs cannot have a depth look into it nor will those small groups be willing to provide the devs with their private hacks.

    Be ensured Garry as well as Facepunch work hard to get Rust cheatfree as far as possible.
    I got banned within 2 days on my main after I tried to test the vis.attack command to see how far it can check cheaters and I did this on my own server in a test enviroment. I do know I shouldn't have used it in the first place but my intention was protecting my own server more and am also supporting the reporting part of rust.

    You should be aware there will never be a full protection against hacks aswell there will never be a undetected hack that lasts for ever.
    In somepoints Unity isn't best for anti-cheat methods. Since if a game based on Unity starts MP without an implemented AntiCheat it is one of the easiest to work around and cheat. From using CE till modify the AssemblyCSharp best example besides Rust would be for example Reign of Kings.

    The BEST possible way would be creating a global banlist database which is controlled by server admins which can identify hacks and handle banning in discret. That is something I am thinking about to create at some point since I would have the hardware and time to maintain such project.
    Developers are already within the most common forums where cheats and hacks are being distributed and discussed on their development.
