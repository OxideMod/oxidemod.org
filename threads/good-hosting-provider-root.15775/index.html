  6. HelloKitty

    Google for winmtr and download it (single exe file. simple as shizits)
    point it to my server at play.swerust.eu and check your traceroutes from you, your admins/mods and friends.
    And even from your current server.
    There you can see a idea of the quality and such.
    Remember that routers can occationaly have high pings even if they give good speeds for the packets it transports .
    Lots of good information with winmtr.
    I am VERY pleased with Hetzner.de 's customer support.
    They have been very friendly, fast and competent so far and i have not a single complaint.

    My server has 350k+ entitys with 70+ players.
    With several MASSIVE bases at more then 30k entitys each.
    it has some bungecord lag at times for some players but i never had any issues myself and at the same time others havent either so..
    It is the players ISP or the connection between server and them.
    And we all know alot of players dont understand the difference between "there" fault and server problems .

    out of the 50+ times i investigated a specific players lag i have had 2 instances of where i had slight packetloss within hetzners network.
    This was the only 2 times i noticed any issues myself also.
    And it was more then likely due to DDOS attacks and the "issue" wasnt bad, and was gone within a few minutes.

    So i defenetly recommend hetzner.
    Check there fixed and custom servers, also there is a "auction" for servers that can be online very fast after they verifyed you as a "genuin customer".
    Verification was done the same day i ordered. i think i actually had my server after just 1-2 hours (first server i ordered from them).
    Windows costs like.. 20 euros i belive (per month). i have 2012 standard and it works fine.
    a very important tip for you is to not really go by the GHZ of the cpu.
    And it isnt really the total cpu performance that you are after, you are after how well 2 cores perform.
    Since rust does not handle more then 2 cores hyperthreading is useless and actually BAD.
    If you know how, then you can disable this in most BIOSes and i have in mine with the free usage of a KVM tool hetzner has (free 3 hours.. took 1 minute ).

    i am unsure if the save is actually faster with SSD for rust, i am using a 3 disk RAID and i would think it is more the cpu that slows down "big" saves rather then it writing 30mb of data to disk .
    But it cant hurt right?

    Ram you are on the correct track to have 16+ gigs.
    im running on 32 gigs, and i restart the rust server instance once each morning to clear the ram usage.

    If you find other providers i would suggest running the winmtr on those hosts aswell on a ip that is running on a customer server (not there main website).

    kimsufi and ovh is nice hosts for budget reasons, but problem is that gameservers demand such intense quality for the packets both speed of transfer (ping speed per packet) and preferebly NEVER any packets lost.
    OVH (owner of kimsufi and so you start) is a highly used and "abused" network so if you can afford a better connection you should get that instead.
    But they still seem popular for gameservers (minecraft is REAL big there due to cheap ram/cpu/disk sizes and its a forgiving game for packetlosses/pings).

    Massive responce but i hope its helpful in some way
