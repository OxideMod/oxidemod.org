    There's a few different places I posted my server, but honestly, best thing to do is to have your server listed on beancan.io and to just keep it online as much as possible, with friends playing on it as often as you can. Players don't always like playing with no one else on the server, but they don't always like playing with 100+ people too. Put your server ip in your signature here like I have and just google "top rust servers" and see what places you can advertise yours. That is what I have done and very slowly I started getting more people popping onto my server. Logging in and introducing yourself doesn't hurt either. Listening to your members helps too. If someone says something isn't working or wishes for something to be added or changed, try your best to do it, within reason. Running a server is like a restaurant, you're serving them something that they have the option to keep returning for more of or not. You need to make it the place they want to come back to in the future. Just keep working to be as clear with your members and let them know what is going on and make things a little easier for them, but not too easy. Like on my server, many members complained of a lack of animals because they needed leather and animal fat. I made a kit that had all the things that come from animals and put it in a kit that is redeemable once per day. It makes it a little more fair for someone. Now, I wouldn't give someone a gun just because they begin bitching about it. Again, within reason. Find a happy medium with your give and take and just be hospitable to your members. The server is great, but if there is nothing that makes them feel like they should keep coming back, why should they?

    Anyway, that aside, for everyone else, I made a video tutorial on how I have installed LustyMap and made it work and tried to streamline it as much as I could and have as much emphasis on the IMPORTANT parts as I could as well.

    Hopefully this helps people out. It isn't hard to install and this should make it even easier.

    You don't use the same API Key for both servers. If they have two separate IP addresses, you have to use two different API Keys. I have never heard of someone's server getting rejected, to be honest. My guess is that means something with the information you provided made it look as if the server is a test server or something. Make sure you make your server title and description unique and that you provide an actual IP address in numbers, not words. (My server, for example, can be connected to using words, but you should provide the numbers). Make all the info you provide as accurate and unique as you can.
