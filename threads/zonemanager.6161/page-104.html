  10. ignignokt84

    @Nogrod, there's a fix for the bug where box zones affect a larger area than expected - can you implement this in the official version?

    ignignokt84 said: ↑
    I believe I've figured out the cause - in method CheckEntities the Physics.OverlapBoxNonAlloc method is passing the full Zone BoxCollider size in, when the method expects HALF size, this causes it to effectively double the size of the zone in all directions from the center.

    In CheckEntities, you can halve the size of the zone. This has solved the issue for me. Can anyone else test this and confirm my results?

    Code:
    entities = Physics.OverlapBoxNonAlloc(Info.Location, Info.Size/2f, colBuffer, Quaternion.Euler(Info.Rotation), playersMask);

  13. Steenamaroo

    I can't think of a way to do that.

    Even if you managed to incorporate player name or group check into the nobuild flag, that would still apply to all zones.
    I think, and I hope I'm not speaking out of turn, you would need to have a custom flag for each of your special zones, if that makes sense.

    As in, if you had three players and three zones, you'd need nobuildExceptPlayerOne, nobuildExceptPlayerTwo, nobuildExceptPlayerThree


    You could modify PermCanbuild to include authlevel > 0 (as it is now) and also members of a group but, again, that wouldn't be zone specific.
    It would just mean chosen players can defeat the nobuild flag in any zone.


    The best solution is probably to deploy tool cabinets, have your guys auth on them, then box them in with a 1x1 undestr true zone per box.
    They may still be susceptible to damage from outside the zone, though...like rockets.
    I'm not sure, but I'd test that.
