Few things about ESP hackers

Discussion in 'Rust Discussion' started by Caffeine, Jul 12, 2016.

  1. Caffeine

    This isn't related to development but maybe useful for server admins and players in-general. I've spoken with hackers and friends of hackers and they've given me a little about of insight about how ESP works with rust.

    - Apparently they can see chests inside of bases but they can't differentiate sizes, so large and small chests look the same, they also can't see what's in them from the outside. Also if you have more than 20 chests apparently this will appear as simply a big blur for some esp hacks. So my advice is put a tremendous amount of empty chests throughout your base, and try to not make your loot room(if you have one) obvious. I did this with a 10x10 base on battlegrounds, and had 20/30 1x1 rooms with a single empty chest and a door, only 1 contained the real loot. Hackers had a very tricky time finding the loot when they offline raided us. (I know this because they told me, and had to destroy 85% of the base to find it)

    - ESP hackers can detect players from a distance and not only that but they can see what items you have on your person. So if you're raiding and have rockets/c4 it's easy for them to find you across the map and lock-on. There's a very popular server admin that has a live monitoring rust.io type system that shows them locking on to people in a distance and helps him ban them. I could link if permitted, is a video.

    - ESP hackers can see hidden stashes, i'm working on a plugin for the public that keeps stats on stashes discovered, by who, and what they got. I figure as admin i'll use the plugin and store extremely hidden stashes around the map to try and bait them out.

  2. sqroot

    In general ESP hackers can see anything their client sees. Rust is built fairly carefully in that regard, so if you want to know whether ESP have access to some sort of information, ask yourself the question whether the client needs to know that piece of information at all times.
    The one with ESP hackers knowing about the inventory of other players was unexpected, though. Are you sure that they can see those? I'd have imagined all containers (inventories, chests) work similarly in that regard: Content is networked when accessed. They can certainly see the stuff that you've got currently equipped (in your hand, on your body) because that needs to be networked for displaying them.

    1) they can see the names of the boxes, so they know which one are where, they can also choose what distance they want to see boxes, so they can still see without getting all blury.
